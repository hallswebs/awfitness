THE COACH

From the Ring to Coaching

Alfie Winter has spent over 15 years in combat sports. As a professional fighter, he learned what it takes to push the human body to its absolute limits — and how to recover, adapt, and come back stronger.

But fighting gave Alfie more than trophies. It gave him a deep understanding of how training should work: progressive, structured, and built around the individual. He saw too many people in gyms doing random workouts with no plan and getting frustrated by their lack of results.

That's why he created AW Fitness — to bring the structured, disciplined approach of professional fight training to everyday people who want real, lasting results.

Alfie's unique approach combines the explosive conditioning of boxing with the functional strength of kettlebell training. It's the same methodology he used to prepare for professional fights, refined and adapted for anyone — regardless of age, fitness level, or experience.

PHILOSOPHY

Alfie's Training Philosophy

Alfie believes that training should be three things: structured, progressive, and sustainable. No random workouts. No burning yourself out in week one. No unrealistic expectations.

His approach is built on these core principles:

  • Structure first — Every session has a purpose. Every week builds on the last. There's a reason for every exercise in the programme.
  • Technique before intensity — Learn to move well before moving fast. Perfect form prevents injury and maximises results.
  • Consistency beats intensity — Three good sessions per week, every week, will always beat seven sessions one week and zero the next.
  • Training should be enjoyable — If you don't enjoy it, you won't stick to it. Boxing and kettlebells are inherently engaging — that's why they work.
  • The mind trains too — Physical training builds mental resilience. Every hard set, every extra rep, every moment you push through discomfort makes you stronger in every area of life.
