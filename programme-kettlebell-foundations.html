WHY KETTLEBELLS

The Ultimate Training Tool

Kettlebells are one of the most effective training tools ever created. A single kettlebell can replace an entire gym — combining strength, cardio, and flexibility work into every session. That's why fighters, military personnel, and elite athletes have used them for decades.

Burn Fat Fast

Kettlebell training burns up to 20 calories per minute — more than running. The combination of strength and cardio in every session creates an enormous metabolic demand that keeps you burning calories hours after training.

Build Functional Strength

Unlike machines, kettlebells train your whole body as one unit. Swings, cleans, and snatches develop explosive hip power, a rock-solid core, and grip strength that transfers directly to everyday life and sport.

Fighter-Level Conditioning

This programme uses AMRAPs, EMOMs, Tabatas, and timed circuits — the same formats used in fight camps. You'll develop the kind of aerobic and anaerobic fitness that lets you push harder for longer.

What Kettlebell Weight Should I Use?

Start lighter than you think — good technique always comes first. Here's a general guide:

Beginner Men 12-16kg
Beginner Women 8-12kg
Intermediate 16-24kg

Weeks 5-6 of this programme increase the weight — so starting with room to progress is key.

8-WEEK PROGRESSION

How You'll Progress

This programme runs on a 2-week rotating schedule with built-in progression at every stage. You'll train 6 different kettlebell sessions across two weeks, plus 2 structured cardio sessions each week. Record your AMRAP rounds and weights used — that's how we measure progress.

Weeks 1-2

Learn Technique

Focus on mastering every movement with perfect form. Go lighter if needed — learn the swings, cleans, presses, snatches, and flows. Record your AMRAP scores as baseline.

Weeks 3-4

Add Volume

Add +2 reps to each AMRAP movement. This builds muscular endurance and pushes your work capacity. Same weight, more work per round.

Weeks 5-6

Increase Load

Move up to a heavier kettlebell. Reset reps to the original numbers. The same sessions now feel completely different — this is where real strength is built.

Weeks 7-8

Beat Your Numbers

With the heavier bell, aim to beat your AMRAP round counts from weeks 1-2. This is the test. Push hard, recover well, and finish strong.

NUTRITION GUIDANCE

Simple Nutrition for Results

You don't need a complicated diet to see results — you need consistency and the right balance. This programme demands high energy output, so we need to fuel your body properly. Below are practical guidelines and a sample training day meal plan to keep things simple.

The Basics — Keep It Simple

Protein at Every Meal

Aim for a palm-sized portion of protein with every meal — chicken, fish, eggs, Greek yoghurt, or a quality whey shake. This supports muscle recovery from your kettlebell sessions.

Earn Your Carbs

Eat your biggest carb portions around training — before and after sessions. Rice, oats, potatoes, and fruit are your best options. On rest days, reduce portion sizes slightly.

Stay Hydrated

Drink at least 2-3 litres of water per day. Add a pinch of salt or an electrolyte tablet on training days. Dehydration kills performance — don't let it happen.

Don't Overthink It

Eat whole, unprocessed food 80% of the time. If it grew in the ground, walked, swam, or flew — eat it. Save the treats for weekends and don't stress about being perfect.

On rest days, drop carb portions by about a third — smaller rice/potato portions at meals 3 and 5. Keep protein the same. This naturally drops you to around 1,900 calories which supports fat loss without compromising recovery.
